Covid-19 pandemic

Covid-19 has been ravaging the world and it is through this pandemic that we shall never take for granted our loved ones as well as our health.

Images from Freepik

First reported death on 9 January 2020

At the start of 2020, there was news about a mystery disease spreading in Wuhan, China. Subsequently, on 11 January 2020, Covid-19 had claimed the life of its first victim, a 61-year-old resident, as China had reported its first confirmed death from this virus.

At that point in time, the virus was not well understood. Countries such as Singapore was acting quickly to screen travellers from Wuhan, and there were fears that the virus can spread quickly and widely during the Chinese New Year as more than 400 million Chinese people would travel around the country, returning to their home towns.

Death polls reach 100 on 28 January 2020

The death polls reached 100 on 28 January, and cases had begun to spread from Wuhan to countries such as Japan, the United States and etc. Wuhan was under virtual lockdown and is under travel curbs. At that point in time, The United States warned against travel to China, and countries such as Japan and South Korea also began evacuating citizens via chartered flights to allow them to return home.

Death polls reach 1,000 on 10 February 2020

The death polls reached 1,000 on 10 February, and the virus has begun its reach worldwide. The virus even claimed the lives of 97 people in China alone in 1 day, which was the highest number of casualties in a day.
Panic ensues as the virus was also spreading rapidly in the cruise ships as hundreds of passengers tested positive for the virus on Diamond Princess quarantined in Japan, and many similar cruise ships were also found to be a hotspot for the virus. 

Death polls reach 10,000 on 20 March 2020

The death polls reached 10,000 on 20 March, and Europe was badly affected by the virus as it had accounted for half of the world's death from this virus. Italy was particularly the worst affected country as it had reached fatalities of 4,032.

There were 250,000 cases worldwide, and 80,000 people had recovered from this virus. Governments such as the United Kingdom had imposed nationwide lockdowns to control the spread of the virus.

Death polls reach 100,000 on 9 April 2020

The death polls reached 100,000 on 9 April, and cases have shot up exponentially. New York's health care system was overwhelmed as there was a tremendous surge in cases, and New York had more cases than any single country.

Just recently, Boris Johnson had tested positive for Covid-19 on 29 March and has come out of intensive care on 9 April.

Large manufacturers such as General Motors, Dyson, and etc., have started manufacturing ventilators to support the treatment of Covid-19 patients by helping to produce medical devices.

Death polls reach 500,000 on 29 June 2020

The death polls reached 500,000 on 29 June, and half of the world's cases have come from Europe and The United States. The virus was also badly affecting South Asia and Africa, and there were worrying concerns since their hospitals may struggle with shortages of medical personnel as well as beds.

The WHO (World Health Organisation) urges governments to implement the right policies to tackle the pandemic. Although progress is made by countries all around the world, the virus is still spreading rapidly.

Death polls reach 1,000,000 on 28 September 2020

The death polls reached 1,000,000 on 28 September, and half of the world's cases have approximately come from The United States, India, and Brazil. Although medical experts have a greater knowledge of the virus, it is still spreading at a fast pace and said that there is no slowing down of the death rates until a vaccine is found and distributed.

Companies such as Johnson & Johnson, Moderna, Pfizer Inc and etc., have also begun trials of their Covid-19 vaccines in this period.

Infection cases reach 40,000,000 on 19 October 2020

The infection cases have reached 40,000,000 on 19 October. New cases are growing every day, and experts believe that unreported cases and death are much higher due to inadequate testing capabilities by countries worldwide as well as under=reporting.

There are countries such as The United Kingdom that implemented a second lockdown to control the spread of the virus.

An official from the WHO (World Health Organisation) believes that the death toll could even reach 2 million before a successful vaccine can be rolled out.

Britain starts mass vaccination on 9 December 2020

Britain has begun mass vaccinating its citizens, and a 90-year-old grandmother is the first to receive the fully-tested Covid-19 shot. It signifies a big leap of progress and hope since the world may be able to look forward to a sense of normalcy soon.

Vaccines such as the Pfizer / BioNTech shot have shown an efficacy rate as high as 95%. Although this is a big victory against fighting the pandemic, people should remain vigilant, wear their masks, respect social distancing rules as this virus is not defeated yet.

Concerns about the side effects of the new vaccines

Concerns about the side effects of the vaccine arise as news has surfaced about cases of fainting and even death from taking the vaccines.

For example, there is a video of a nurse fainting after receiving a Covid-19 vaccine, and there was speculation that the vaccine will cause mass genocide. The claim was disproven as the nurse has a medical condition where she faints when she feels pain, and the vaccine has undergone safety testing.

Furthermore, the US government agencies have analysed the safety data of these vaccines and have ensured that the vaccine is safe before it is approved for use. However, there will be some side effects such as tiredness, headache and etc.

Concerns about the rise of the new coronavirus variant

Concerns about the rise of the new coronavirus variant arise as it is rapidly replacing other versions of the virus, mutate important components of the virus as well as significantly increase its infectiousness .

It is currently spreading rapidly in the United Kingdom and cases have arise in countries such as Denmark, Australia, Singapore and etc. This caused more than 40 countries to ban air travel from the United Kingdom.

It is unclear if this variant of the virus will make infections more deadly. However the vaccine will still be effective as it enables an immune response to the virus.
